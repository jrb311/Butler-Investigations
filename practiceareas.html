Practice Areas

Butler Investigations has extensive experience conducting background investigations on people and organizations in high stakes criminal and administrative matters where the credibility of a witness - or lack thereof - can make or break the case. John corroborates all due diligence findings through multiple sources, using a combination of database searches, open-source intelligence techniques, and field work. This diligient process maximizes our ability to provide accurate, comprehensive, and detailed results to your organization.

Butler Investigations has a proven track record of locating hard-to-find witnesses. For example, John regularly located witnesses in decades-old criminal matters as staff investigator at the Pennsylvania Innocence Project. In such cases, witnesses had relocated, changed jobs, and even changed identities, and the leads to finding them were few and far between. John knows that sucessfully tracking down witnesses depends on a number of investigative methods, including open source intelligence gathering, public records searches, and field work.

John Butler has interviewed and taken written statements from hundreds of witnesses in high stakes and sensitive circumstances. He understands that the witnesses involved in his cases are human beings with personal - and often tragic - stories to tell, and he endeavors to make them feel heard and appreciated for their contributions to the case. For Butler Investigations, conducting interviews is about more than just asking questions; it is about developing relationships and building trust. This approach ensures that your organization will receieve the best quality information possible from any human source in your case.
